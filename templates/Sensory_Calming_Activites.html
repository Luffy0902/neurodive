Sensory Calming Activities

What is Overstimulation?

Overstimulation is a state of overwhelmedness caused by your senses recieving too much sensory information, making it difficult to process the information. Overcoming overstimulation/sensory overload often involves removing or changing a stimulant from the environment, or removing yourself from the environment. This helps your brain recieve less sensory information.

Visual Information

Visual input can cause overstimulation. A common visual stimulant that makes people uncomfortable is bright lighting. To reduce visual overstimulation through lighting you can:

  • Move to a dark room
  • Use soft and dim lighting to create a calming environment
  • Embrace natural daylight to improve your mood
  • Use candles and twinkle/fairy lights for a gentle lighting experience
  • Wear light filtering glasses

Visual stimulators are important for calming down.

  • Find a relaxing view of nature such as a waterfall
  • Use soft and muted colors in the environment as they are less stimulating than bright colors
  • Look at or watch calming artwork, photos, and videos to relax
  • Enjoy objects like lava lamps that distract from the environment
  • Wear light filtering glasses

Auditory Information

Auditory information is what you hear. Loud or unpleasant noises such as a large group of people talking at once can overstimulate people. To overcome auditory overstimulation:

  • Listen to calming and relaxing music
  • Wear noise cancelling headphones
  • Listen to nature sounds
  • Listen to white noise
  • Watch ASMR videos
  • Do guided meditation to relax
  • Move to a quiet room

Olfactory Information

Olfactory information is what you smell. Some smells are more pleasant and suitable to be around. To overcome olfactory overstimulation:

  • Wear a face mask to mask unpleasant smells
  • Find the source of the disturbing smell and remove or substitute it
  • Open a window to keep the room well ventilated
  • Smell calming aromas such as lavender, chamomile, or anything you particularly find calming

Gustatory Information

Gustatory information is what you eat. People can have certain "safe foods" and "comfort foods" which they prefer to eat in certain circumstances.

  • Eat a food that brings joy and comfort
  • Drink liquids like hot chocolate that bring relaxation
  • Avoid certain textures in foods

Tactile Information

Tactile information is what you feel. Certain objects can reduce overstimulation.

  • Engage in tactile hobbies such as crocheting and knitting
  • Use fidget toyssuch as fidget spinners
  • Play around with different textures with objects like kinetic sand or a fluffy rug
  • Do touch-based activitiessuch as yoga
  • Wear comfy clothes

Thermal Information

Things like the weather and temperature can affect our mood. To sooth yourself, you can:

  • Turn on air conditioning or fan to cool the environment
  • Turn on a heater to warm the environment <
  • Wrap yourself in a warm blanket
  • Wear suitable clothes to fit the environment
  • Eat hot or cold foods and beverages
  • Take a warm or cold shower or bath

Don't forget...

It's important to involve movement in your daily routine. Keep a decluttered and cozy environment designed to fit your personal needs. It's also important to have support from friends, family, or support groups and communities.
